How It Works

From Record Chaos to Airworthiness Clarity in Real Time

Every aircraft transition processed by Zeto Aeroguard follows a proven four-stage intelligence journey from ingesting fragmented legacy documentation and building a live Transactional Digital Twin, to generating compliance gap reports and delivering blockchain-backed handover sign-offs with full audit traceability.

STEP 01

Ingest & Digitise Records

Zeto Aeroguard's AI-powered OCR engine processes thousands of pages of handwritten maintenance logs, engineering orders, and certification forms extracting critical data with 94% accuracy using LayoutLM and Transformer models trained on aviation-specific documentation.

STEP 02

Build the Digital Twin

A living Transactional Digital Twin of the aircraft is constructed in real time mapping every maintenance event, component swap, and structural repair against specific lease redelivery conditions and financial liability thresholds, identifying compliance gaps months before transition.

STEP 03

Compliance Gap Analysis

The Regulatory Friction Index (RFI) module automatically compares the aircraft's current configuration against the target registry's requirements UK CAA, EASA, or FAA predicting the exact cost and time needed to achieve full compliance before transition.

STEP 04

Blockchain Handover & Sign-Off

All document verifications and technical sign-offs are recorded on a private blockchain ledger creating an immutable chain of evidence that eliminates "missing record" disputes and provides complete certainty to all parties throughout the handover process.

Our Solutions

Six Integrated Platform Modules

Zeto Aeroguard delivers a fully integrated aviation asset intelligence ecosystem AI document extraction, compliance mapping, risk scoring, blockchain trust, scenario simulation, and hangar-side edge processing designed to eliminate fragmented transitions and make aircraft handovers predictable, transparent, and instantaneous.

AI Core

AI Technical Records Engine

OCR and NLP models extract critical data from legacy handwritten aviation logbooks and maintenance release certificates with 94% accuracy.

Built using optimised LayoutLM and DenseNet architectures fine-tuned on aviation-specific "dirty fingerprint" handwritten records. The engine processes up to 50,000 pages per aircraft, verifying Back-to-Birth (BTB) traces for Life-Limited Parts (LLPs), structural repair maps, and AD/SB compliance a core barrier to entry for any competitor.

Core AI

Transactional Digital Twin Engine

A living virtual replica of the aircraft's technical and legal status, mapping every maintenance event against real-time lease redelivery conditions.

The Transactional Digital Twin Engine interprets every maintenance event, component swap, and structural repair as linguistic and financial data mapped in real time against specific lease redelivery conditions. Using CNN and Transformer-based networks, it identifies airworthiness compliance issues months before an asset is grounded for audit, converting passive records into active financial intelligence.

Compliance

Regulatory Friction Index (RFI)

An automated compliance mapper that predicts the time and cost required to migrate an aircraft between global registries UK CAA, EASA, and FAA.

The RFI module compares the aircraft's current configuration against the requirements of the importing regulatory authority, computing a gap-analysis score across Airworthiness Directives, modifications, and certification status. It generates instant "Time-to-Registry" projections, enabling owners to resolve gaps while the aircraft still generates revenue eliminating costly last-minute discoveries that derail sales agreements.

Trust Layer

Blockchain Handover Ledger

A private blockchain records every document upload, verification, and technical sign-off creating an immutable chain of evidence for all parties.

Zeto Aeroguard's blockchain ledger records every document upload, verification step, and technical sign-off with a tamper-proof timestamp. This creates an immutable "chain of evidence" that eliminates "missing record" disputes that plague traditional aircraft transitions. The ledger is GDPR-compliant, ISO 27001-aligned, and specifically engineered for the high-stakes legal environment of aircraft ownership migration.

Risk Intelligence

Asset Stability Score

The industry's first quantitative metric for documentation quality allowing lessors and financiers to price risk objectively rather than via qualitative spot-checks.

The "Asset Stability Score" analyses the lineage and certification (Form 1/8130) of every major structural repair, assigning an objective, defensible measure of technical integrity. This score directly influences the asset's market valuation, insurance premiums, and resale potential providing financiers and insurers with the first data-driven basis for asset-backed securitisation pricing in aviation.

Edge Hardware

Hangar Edge Hub

Pre-configured onsite hardware units deploying AI inference locally in secure, low-connectivity hangar environments ensuring data sovereignty at the point of maintenance.

Unlike cloud-only systems that require sensitive records to be exported to external servers, Zeto Aeroguard's Hangar Edge Hubs process high-volume technical records locally using NVIDIA Jetson Orin edge processors. This ensures 100% data sovereignty and 24/7 operational capability even in low-connectivity hangar environments, integrating seamlessly with AMOS and TRAX via RESTful APIs.

Common Questions

What aircraft types does Zeto Aeroguard currently support?

+
The current prototype is validated for narrow-body commercial aircraft (Airbus A320/A321 family and Boeing 737 family), which represent the highest volume of global aircraft transitions. Year 2 development will expand the platform to wide-body aircraft (Airbus A350, Boeing 787) and high-value engine portfolios (CFM LEAP, Pratt & Whitney GTF, Rolls-Royce Trent). The modular AI architecture allows rapid adaptation across new asset classes, including business jets and helicopters.

How does Zeto Aeroguard achieve 94% OCR accuracy on handwritten records?

+
Unlike generic OCR tools, our AI engine is purpose-built for aviation technical documentation. We use LayoutLM and DenseNet architectures fine-tuned on thousands of "dirty fingerprint" handwritten maintenance logs, engineering orders, and certification forms specific to commercial aviation. The models understand aviation-specific vocabulary, abbreviations, part numbers, and structural repair notation context that generic OCR tools entirely miss. Continuous learning from new data ensures the models improve with every transition processed.

Is Zeto Aeroguard compliant with UK CAA CAP 1330 digital record standards?

+
Yes. The platform is specifically engineered to align with the UK Civil Aviation Authority's CAP 1330 Digital Technical Records framework, as well as EASA Part-CAMO record-keeping requirements and ATA Spec 2500 (Aircraft Transfer Records). The system is also GDPR-compliant, ISO 27001-aligned, and working towards Cyber Essentials Plus certification. All data is processed in UK-based cloud environments (Microsoft Azure UK South or AWS London) to maintain full data sovereignty.

What return on investment can a lessor expect from using Zeto Aeroguard?

+
The financial case is compelling. A standard 60-day aircraft transition audit delay represents a direct loss of up to £2.7 million in foregone revenue (at £15,000£45,000 per day). Zeto Aeroguard reduces manual audit hours by 70% and transition audit costs by up to 50%, enabling aircraft to return to revenue-generating service weeks faster. For a Tier-1 lessor managing 20 aircraft transitions per year, the platform typically delivers annual savings many times greater than the subscription cost.

How does the blockchain ledger work for multi-party handovers?

+
Zeto Aeroguard uses a private blockchain ledger not a public cryptocurrency network to record every document upload, verification step, and technical sign-off during an aircraft handover. Each action is time-stamped and cryptographically signed by the relevant authorised party (lessor, lessee, CAMO, or MRO). This creates an immutable chain of evidence that all parties can independently verify, eliminating the "missing record" disputes that frequently result in multimillion-pound legal claims during adversarial aircraft redeliveries.
