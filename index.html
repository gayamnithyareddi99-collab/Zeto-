How It Works

From Record Chaos to Active Asset Liquidity in Real Time

Every aircraft transition processed by ZATO-AeroGuard follows a proven four-stage Active Intelligence journey — from ingesting fragmented legacy documentation and constructing a live Transactional Digital Twin, to generating Regulatory Friction Index reports and delivering blockchain-backed handover sign-offs with cryptographically timestamped audit traceability. The result: "First-Time-Right" audits that eliminate the £2.7M dead-time cost per asset.

STEP 01 — TRANSITION

Ingest & Digitise Records

ZATO-AeroGuard's AI-powered OCR engine processes up to 50,000 pages of handwritten maintenance logs, engineering orders, and certification forms per aircraft — extracting critical data with 94%+ accuracy using LayoutLM and CNN models fine-tuned on aviation-specific "dirty fingerprint" documentation. No record too complex, no logbook too degraded.

STEP 02 — DIVERSIFY

Build the Transactional Digital Twin

A living Transactional Digital Twin of the aircraft is constructed in real time — mapping every maintenance event, component swap, and structural repair against specific lease redelivery conditions and financial liability thresholds. Supports narrow-body, wide-body, and NewGen LEAP/GTF engine complexities, identifying compliance gaps months before transition.

STEP 03 — COMPLIANCE

Regulatory Friction Index (RFI)

The Regulatory Friction Index module automatically cross-maps the aircraft's configuration against the target registry's requirements — UK CAA, EASA, or FAA — predicting re-registration timelines and identifying "compliance clashes" that carry six-figure-per-week penalties if undetected. Converts reactive firefighting into proactive financial planning.

STEP 04 — GROW

Blockchain Handover & Sign-Off

All document verifications and technical sign-offs are recorded on a private, permissioned blockchain ledger with cryptographic timestamps — creating an immutable "Chain of Evidence" that ends "missing record" disputes and delivers ABS-readiness for aviation banks with objective stability metrics. Full data sovereignty. Full institutional trust.

About ZATO-AeroGuard

The UK's First "Active Intelligence" Aviation Asset Transition Platform

ZATO-AeroGuard is a UK-based aviation technology company pioneering the world's first Transactional Digital Twin platform for aircraft ownership transfers and lease redeliveries. Built on a foundation of deep operational aviation expertise, the platform addresses the systemic "Efficiency and Liquidity Crisis" that costs the global aviation industry billions of pounds annually through aircraft transition downtime — where a single 60-day audit delay translates to £2.7 million in lost revenue per asset.

The platform combines advanced computer vision (LayoutLM, CNN), natural language processing, and blockchain technology to convert fragmented maintenance records into real-time airworthiness and financial liability models — enabling lessors, airlines, and MROs to achieve zero-downtime aircraft transitions with full regulatory compliance. Distinct UI modules serve high-value assets including LEAP/GTF engines and Power-by-the-Hour (PBH) complexities.

ZATO-AeroGuard is fully aligned with the UK Civil Aviation Authority's push for Digital Technical Records (CAP 1330) and has achieved Technology Readiness Level 7, with a functional prototype validated in a real-world MRO hangar environment. The platform is engineered to signal institutional readiness to aviation banks, insurers, and global lessors from the very first engagement.

The Six Proprietary Engines

Active Asset Liquidity — Six Integrated Intelligence Modules

ZATO-AeroGuard delivers a fully integrated "Active Asset Liquidity" ecosystem — moving beyond passive record storage into live financial intelligence. Engine 01 — AI Core

AI Technical Records Engine

OCR and NLP models extract critical data from legacy handwritten aviation logbooks and maintenance release certificates with 94%+ accuracy — processing up to 50,000 MRO pages per aircraft using LayoutLM and CNN architectures.

Financial Impact: Eliminates manual audit bottlenecks; reduces labour hours by 70%, accelerating return-to-service timelines and protecting £15,000–£45,000/day in lease revenue. Built using optimised LayoutLM and DenseNet architectures fine-tuned on aviation-specific "dirty fingerprint" handwritten records. The engine verifies Back-to-Birth (BTB) traces for Life-Limited Parts (LLPs), structural repair maps, and AD/SB compliance — a core barrier to entry for any competitor.

Engine 02 — Core AI

Transactional Digital Twin Engine

A living virtual replica of the aircraft's technical and legal status, mapping every maintenance event against real-time lease redelivery conditions — powered by CNN and Transformer networks processing 50,000+ MRO pages.

Financial Impact: Eliminates manual audit bottlenecks; reduces labour hours by 70%, accelerating return-to-service timelines for global lessors managing multi-jurisdictional assets. The Transactional Digital Twin Engine interprets every maintenance event, component swap, and structural repair as linguistic and financial data — mapped in real time against specific lease redelivery conditions. It identifies airworthiness compliance issues months before an asset is grounded for audit, converting passive records into active financial intelligence.

Engine 03 — Compliance

Regulatory Friction Index (RFI)

Automated cross-border compliance mapping across UK CAA, EASA, and FAA registries — predicting re-registration timelines and identifying "compliance clashes" before they become six-figure-per-week penalties.

Financial Impact: Predicts re-registration timelines and identifies "compliance clashes," preventing penalties of six figures per week — protecting asset sale agreements and ABS pricing integrity. The RFI module compares the aircraft's current configuration against the requirements of the importing regulatory authority, computing a gap-analysis score across Airworthiness Directives, modifications, and certification status. Instant "Time-to-Registry" projections enable owners to resolve gaps while the aircraft still generates revenue.

Engine 04 — Trust Layer

Blockchain Handover Ledger

A private, permissioned, cryptographically timestamped blockchain records every document upload, verification, and technical sign-off — creating an immutable "Chain of Evidence" for adversarial aircraft transitions.

Financial Impact: Creates an immutable "Chain of Evidence" for adversarial transitions, ending "missing record" disputes that frequently result in multimillion-pound legal claims. ZATO-AeroGuard's blockchain ledger is GDPR-compliant, ISO 27001-aligned, and specifically engineered for the high-stakes legal environment of aircraft ownership migration. All parties — lessor, lessee, CAMO, or MRO — can independently verify every sign-off, delivering full ABS-readiness for aviation banks and insurers.

Engine 05 — Risk Intelligence

Asset Stability Score (ASS)

40+ weighted parameters analysing repair robustness — the industry's first quantitative metric for documentation quality, providing objective risk metrics for Asset-Backed Securitisation (ABS) and protecting multimillion-pound residual values.

Financial Impact: Provides objective risk metrics for Asset-Backed Securitisation (ABS), protecting multimillion-pound residual values and enabling data-driven insurance premium pricing. The Asset Stability Score analyses the lineage and certification (Form 1/8130) of every major structural repair across 40+ weighted parameters, assigning a defensible measure of technical integrity. This score directly influences the asset's market valuation, insurance premiums, and resale potential — the first data-driven basis for ABS pricing in aviation.

Engine 06 — Edge Hardware & Sovereignty

Hangar Edge AI Hub

Offline-capable, local edge-AI inference modules deployed at MRO facilities — ensuring 24/7 data sovereignty and audit continuity in low-connectivity hangar environments, integrating with AMOS, TRAX, and Ultramain via secure RESTful APIs.

Financial Impact: Ensures data sovereignty and 24/7 audit continuity in low-connectivity MRO environments, eliminating cloud dependency risk and protecting sensitive airworthiness records. Common Questions About ZATO-AeroGuard

What aircraft types does ZATO-AeroGuard currently support?

+
The current TRL-7 validated prototype supports narrow-body commercial aircraft (Airbus A320/A321 family and Boeing 737 family), representing the highest volume of global aircraft transitions. Year 2 development expands to wide-body aircraft (Airbus A350, Boeing 787) and high-value NewGen engine portfolios (CFM LEAP, Pratt & Whitney GTF, Rolls-Royce Trent). How does ZATO-AeroGuard achieve 94%+ OCR accuracy on handwritten records?

+
Unlike generic OCR tools, ZATO-AeroGuard's AI engine is purpose-built for aviation technical documentation using LayoutLM and DenseNet architectures fine-tuned on thousands of "dirty fingerprint" handwritten maintenance logs, engineering orders, and certification forms specific to commercial aviation. The models understand aviation-specific vocabulary, abbreviations, part numbers, and structural repair notation — context that generic OCR tools entirely miss. This is the operational intelligence that Ronak Mistry's 9.5+ years of on-the-wing MRO experience directly trained into the models. Continuous learning from new data ensures models improve with every transition processed.

Is ZATO-AeroGuard compliant with UK CAA CAP 1330 digital record standards?

+
Yes. The platform is specifically engineered to align with the UK Civil Aviation Authority's CAP 1330 Digital Technical Records framework, EASA Part-CAMO record-keeping requirements, and ATA Spec 2500 (Aircraft Transfer Records). The system is GDPR-compliant with airworthiness-grade encryption, ISO 27001-aligned, and operates within the UK AI Regulation Framework ensuring human-in-the-loop transparency for all high-stakes airworthiness decisions. All data is processed in UK-based cloud environments (Microsoft Azure UK South or AWS London) to maintain full data sovereignty.

The financial case is compelling. A standard 60-day aircraft transition audit delay represents a direct loss of up to £2.7 million in foregone revenue at £15,000–£45,000 per day. ZATO-AeroGuard reduces manual audit hours by 70% and transition audit costs by up to 50%, enabling aircraft to return to revenue-generating service weeks faster. How does the blockchain ledger work for multi-party handovers?

+
ZATO-AeroGuard uses a private, permissioned blockchain — not a public cryptocurrency network — to record every document upload, verification step, and technical sign-off during an aircraft handover. Each action is cryptographically timestamped and signed by the relevant authorised party (lessor, lessee, CAMO, or MRO). This creates an immutable "Chain of Evidence" that all parties can independently verify, eliminating "missing record" disputes that frequently result in multimillion-pound legal claims during adversarial aircraft redeliveries. The ledger is GDPR-compliant and ISO 27001-aligned for the high-stakes legal environment of aircraft ownership migration.
